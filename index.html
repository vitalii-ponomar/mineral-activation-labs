Why this matters now

Industry is under pressure to cut emissions, reduce landfill, and extract more value from mineral resources and byproducts. Yet many materials remain underused because decisions are often based on incomplete qualification. Chemistry alone is rarely enough. Practical decisions need mineralogical, processing, and performance insight.

01

Underused feedstocks

Clays, residues, slags, and treated soils may hold value, but their real potential is often unclear.

02

Uncertain pathways

Even promising materials can fail without the right activation strategy, application fit, and testing sequence.

Need for translation

Technical data must be translated into practical next steps for product development, pilots, and decision-making.

What we do

We help partners understand what a material is, what it could become, and what should happen next.

Feedstock qualification

Assess chemistry, mineralogy, reactivity, and practical technical potential of mineral and industrial side streams.

Activation pathway design

Define technically grounded routes such as calcination, alkaline activation, blending, or hybrid processing.

Pilot strategy support

Structure the pathway from early screening to test plans, application fit, and pilot-ready questions.

Who we work with

Mineral activation is not one market. It is a capability that creates value across multiple material-intensive sectors.

Cement and concrete

Lower-cement blends, supplementary cementitious materials, calcined clays, activated binders, and non-traditional mineral feedstocks.

Mining and mineral processing

Residue valorisation, byproduct qualification, and strategic pathways for underused mineral streams.

Waste and remediation

Infrastructure and innovation

Science-backed assessment of alternative material pathways for lower-carbon construction and circular resource use.
